If you are seeking a cost-effective method to pave your driveway, parking lot, or rural road, asphalt millings (recycled asphalt) may be the ideal solution. They are significantly less expensive than new asphalt while still offering a robust and durable surface.

### **How Much Do Asphalt Millings Cost?**

- Price Range: $0.50 – $2.50 per square foot (In comparison, new asphalt costs between $2 and $5 per square foot.)

- Factors That Influence Costs:

  - Location (prices may vary by region).

  - The typical thickness ranges from 2 to 4 inches.

  - Delivery fees for bulk purchases.

  - Installation: DIY vs. Hiring a Contractor

### **Why Choose Asphalt Millings?**

✔ **Cost-Effective** – Saves 50% or more compared to new asphalt.
✔ **Environmentally Friendly** – Reuses existing pavement, reducing waste.

### **Where Can You Utilize Them?**

- Driveways: Ideal for Homes and Farms

- Parking Lots: A Cost-Saving Solution for Businesses

- Gravel Roads (towns utilize them for maintenance).

### **How to Obtain the Best Price**

1. **Buy in Bulk** – More cost-effective per ton for larger areas.

2. **Compare Local Suppliers** - Explore Paving Companies in Your Area.

3. **DIY If Possible** – Renting a compactor is more cost-effective than hiring a professional.

### **Final Tip**

Asphalt millings are an excellent choice for those seeking a cost-effective and durable paving solution. In most areas, prices remain below $2 per square foot, making them one of the most affordable paving options available.
